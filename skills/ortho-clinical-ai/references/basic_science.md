# Orthopaedic Basic Science Quick Reference

## Biomechanics Concepts

### Stress-Strain Curve
- **Elastic region**: Linear relationship, material returns to original shape
- **Yield point**: Transition from elastic to plastic deformation
- **Plastic region**: Permanent deformation
- **Ultimate stress**: Maximum stress before failure
- **Failure point**: Material breaks

### Viscoelastic Properties (Critical for Exam)
| Property | Definition | Clinical Example |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Creep** | Increasing displacement under constant load over time | Ligament stretching under sustained force |
| **Stress Relaxation** | Decreasing stress under constant displacement over time | Plaster cast loosening |
| **Hysteresis** | Area between loading and unloading curves (energy dissipated) | Articular cartilage energy absorption |

### Key Formulas
- **Stress (σ)** = Force / Area
- **Strain (ε)** = Change in length / Original length
- **Young's Modulus (E)** = Stress / Strain (stiffness)
- **Moment of Inertia** = Resistance to bending (depends on geometry)
- **Polar Moment** = Resistance to torsion

### Bone Mechanical Properties
- Strongest in **compression**
- Weakest in **tension** and **shear**
- Anisotropic (direction-dependent properties)
- Viscoelastic (rate-dependent behavior)

## Biomaterials

### Bone Graft Properties
| Property | Definition | Examples |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Osteogenic** | Contains living bone cells | Autograft (cancellous bone) |
| **Osteoinductive** | Stimulates stem cells to differentiate into osteoblasts | BMPs, DBM (demineralized bone matrix) |
| **Osteoconductive** | Provides scaffold for bone growth | Hydroxyapatite, TCP, allograft |

### Important: Osteoinductive Substances
- **Demineralized Bone Matrix (DBM)** — Contains BMPs, is osteoinductive
- **Beta-tricalcium phosphate (β-TCP)** — Osteoconductive only
- **Hydroxyapatite (HA)** — Osteoconductive only
- **Silicone** — Neither osteoinductive nor osteoconductive

### Bearing Surfaces (Arthroplasty)
| Combination | Wear Rate | Notes |
|------------|-----------|-------|
| Metal-on-Poly | Moderate | Standard, improved with XLPE |
| Ceramic-on-Poly | Low | Good option |
| Ceramic-on-Ceramic | Very low | Squeaking risk |
| Metal-on-Metal | Variable | Pseudotumor risk, declining use |

## Connective Tissue Biology

### Tendons vs Ligaments
| Feature | Tendons | Ligaments |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| **Primary collagen** | Type I | Type I (but less total collagen) |
| **Collagen content** | Higher (~86% dry weight) | Lower (~70% dry weight) |
| **Water content** | Lower | Higher |
| **Proteoglycans** | Lower | Higher |
| **Elastin content** | Lower (~2%) | Higher (~5%) |
| **Organization** | Highly parallel, hierarchical | Less ordered, multidirectional |
| **Vascularity** | Relatively avascular | Moderate vascularity |
| **Function** | Transmit muscle force to bone | Connect bone to bone |

### Collagen Types
| Type | Location |
|------|----------|
| **Type I** | Bone, tendon, ligament, skin (most abundant) |
| **Type II** | Hyaline cartilage, nucleus pulposus |
| **Type III** | Healing tissue, blood vessels |
| **Type IV** | Basement membrane |
| **Type V** | Cell surfaces, interstitial tissue |

## Bone Healing

### Primary (Direct) Healing
- Requires absolute stability (compression plating)
- No callus formation
- Contact healing (cutting cones) or gap healing

### Secondary (Indirect) Healing
- Relative stability (IM nailing, cast)
- Callus formation
- Phases: Inflammation → Soft callus → Hard callus → Remodeling

### Factors Affecting Healing
**Promote**: Mechanical stability, adequate blood supply, growth factors (BMPs, PDGF)
**Impair**: NSAIDs (controversial), smoking, infection, excessive motion, malnutrition

## Musculoskeletal Oncology

### Good Prognostic Factors in Osteosarcoma
- **Parosteal variant** (best prognosis among subtypes)
- **>90% tumor necrosis** after neoadjuvant chemotherapy (Huvos grading)
- Extremity (vs axial) location
- No metastases at presentation
- No skip lesions

### Poor Prognostic Factors
- Small cell variant
- Skip lesions
- Inadequate (<90%) necrosis
- Axial location
- Metastatic disease at presentation
- Age <10 or >40

### Huvos Grading (Tumor Necrosis Response)
| Grade | Necrosis | Prognosis |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| I | Little/no effect | Poor |
| II | 50-90% necrosis | Moderate |
| III | >90% necrosis | Good |
| IV | 100% necrosis | Excellent |
