# Orthopaedic Classification Systems Quick Reference

## Fracture Classifications

### Universal
| System | Application | Grades |
|--------|------------|--------|
| **AO/OTA** | All fractures | Type-Group-Subgroup (e.g., 31-A1.2) |
| **Gustilo-Anderson** | Open fractures | I, II, IIIA, IIIB, IIIC |
| **Tscherne** | Closed soft tissue | C0, C1, C2, C3 |

### Upper Extremity
| System | Application | Key Details |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **Neer** | Proximal humerus | 2/3/4 part based on displaced segments |
| **Mason** | Radial head | I (undisplaced), II (marginal), III (comminuted), IV (+ dislocation) |
| **Gartland** | Supracondylar humerus | I (undisplaced), II (displaced, intact posterior cortex), III (completely displaced) |
| **Milch** | Lateral condyle humerus | I (lateral to trochlear groove), II (through groove) |

### Lower Extremity
| System | Application | Key Details |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **Garden** | Femoral neck | I-IV (based on trabecular alignment) |
| **Pauwels** | Femoral neck | I (<30°), II (30-50°), III (>50°) angle |
| **Evans/Jensen** | Intertrochanteric | Stable vs unstable patterns |
| **Schatzker** | Tibial plateau | I-VI |
| **Pilon** | Tibial plafond | Ruedi-Allgower I-III |
| **Danis-Weber** | Ankle | A (below syndesmosis), B (at), C (above) |
| **Lauge-Hansen** | Ankle | Mechanism-based: SAD, SER, PAB, PER |

## Exam High-Yield Associations

### Radial Club Hand Associations
- VACTERL syndrome
- Holt-Oram syndrome (cardiac + radial ray defect)
- Fanconi's anemia (pancytopenia)
- TAR syndrome (thrombocytopenia-absent radius)
- NOT associated: Larsen syndrome (multiple joint dislocations)

### Pavlik Harness Complications
- Femoral nerve palsy (most common nerve injury)
- Inferior dislocation (if worn incorrectly)
- Worsening acetabular dysplasia
- AVN of femoral head
- NOT typically: Sciatic nerve palsy

### Female Athlete Triad
1. Eating disorder / relative energy deficiency
2. Amenorrhea / menstrual dysfunction
3. Osteoporosis / low bone mineral density
- Note: Stress fracture is a consequence, NOT part of the triad

### Limb Length Discrepancy Prediction Methods
- Multiplier method (Paley)
- Moseley straight-line graph
- Green-Anderson growth remaining tables
- NOT: Ratliff's method (used for AVN classification in pediatric femoral neck fractures)

### Tarsal Coalition Radiographic Signs
- C-sign (talocalcaneal coalition)
- Anteater nose sign (calcaneonavicular coalition)
- Talar beaking (dorsal osteophyte)
- Osseous bar visible on CT
- Absent middle facet on CT (talocalcaneal coalition)
